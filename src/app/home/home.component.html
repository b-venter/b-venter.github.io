About me

I have been involved from the ground up in telecommunications, starting as an apprentice and doing copper cabling for PBX and LAN installations. I have worked on enterprise communications systems like the Ericsson MD110 up to its present form: Mitel's MXONE. SIP has supplanted H.323, IP has replaced TDM, and proprietary hardware has given way to containers: communication technology has moved from copper to cloud, and it has not always been predictable.

I currently consult for companies desiring to implement full collaboration solutions and contact centers. I run my own matrix homeserver, dabble in web development - previously with LAMP, but more recently with Angular, Echo and ArangoDB - and continually look to improve my programming ability. In between are many things which catch my curiosity. This blog records these digital encounters.
