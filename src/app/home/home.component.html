About me

SIP has supplanted H.323 ... IP has replaced TDM ... and proprietary hardware has given way to open source: communication technology has moved from copper to cloud, and it has not always been predictable.

I have been involved from the ground up in telecommunications, starting as an apprentice and doing copper cabling for PBX and LAN installations, I have enjoyed working on enterprise communications systems like the Ericsson MD110 up to its present form: Mitel's MXONE. Collaboration tools, contact centers and more have been thrown into the mix. Besides running my own matrix homeserver, I enjoy web development - previously with LAMP, but more recently with Angular, Go and ArangoDB - and continually look to improve my programming ability. In between are many things which catch my curiosity. This blog records all these digital encounters.

I currently consult for companies desiring to implement full collaboration solutions and contact centers. Should you wish to consult me, feel free to get in touch by means of my LinkedIn profile.
